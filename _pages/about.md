I am Yanis Seyifou, a student in the Magistère of Fundamental Physics at Paris-Saclay University. I am deeply passionate about theoretical and mathematical physics.

I recently completed my first research internship at the IJCLab, where I had the opportunity to immerse myself in the world of theoretical research. My work focused on studying two-loop sunrise-type Feynman integrals. Specifically, I used the Mellin-Barnes representation to find exact expressions for these integrals in terms of hypergeometric functions. This highly theoretical experience fully confirmed my vocation for research.

I also enjoy developing my practical and numerical skills, having worked on projects ranging from experimental lock-exchange flows to the numerical resolution of the thin plate equation to visualize Chladni figures.

Next year (2026–2027), I will be joining the Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität (LMU) in Munich as an Erasmus+ exchange student, where I will follow an advanced curriculum focused on mathematics and theoretical physics. Following this, I aim to join a high-level Master's program—such with the long-term goal of pursuing a Ph.D. in mathematical and theoretical physics.

Beyond physics, I am heavily invested in dance and music. I practice breakdance with the university association ASESCO and perform with the semi-professional company Art'Corps Créative. This demanding engagement provides me with an essential balance, combining rigor, self-improvement, and creativity.
